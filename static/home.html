About

MERCURY MIS

Department of Pollution Control and Environmental Health

  • INTRODUCTION
  • PROJECT
  • SYSTEM

The Federal government of Nigeria is desirous to implement the provisions of the Minamata Convention; a global treaty to protect human health and the environment from the adverse effects of mercury. Thus, the Federal government through the Federal Ministry of Environment undertook a Minamata Convention on Mercury Initial Assessment (MIA) from 2015-2017.

The report highlights priority actions to be taken, part of which is to develop detailed sectoral action plans to implement the Convention in Nigeria and, within such action plans, implement activities that would assist the nation to achieve its sustainable sound management of chemicals.

This project is building on the Minamata Convention Initial Assessment (MIA) and aims to develop Specific National Action plans for the cement, mercury in products, trade and supply, waste and environment and health sectors in close consultation with relevant stakeholders and these will be incorporated into the National implementation strategy.

The Mercury Management Information System is developed with a view to collect, process, store data and share information working collaboratively with various MDAs. This system will help the focal point to access information/data collected in the field and use the same to ensure up to date information are made available remotely to relevant stakeholders. The Nigeria government leveraged on the provisions under article 14 of the Minamata convention, hence, through the Focal point engages its international partners, multilaterally and bilaterally, to develop this system, which is meant to address key mercury issues such as: • Mercury data collection and inventory development • Mercury source characterization, and • Mercury best practices for emissions and use reduction.
